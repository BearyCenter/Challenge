System Shift

How dark mood returns without losing the key

Light Foundation

Still clean and brand-ready

Ivory layouts, pastel cards, and navy typography remain the base system for websites, apps, and collateral.

Dark Reintroduction

Applied only where emotion matters

Reintroduce dark mood in heroes, campaign moments, villa image overlays, testimonials, and premium experience modules.

Hero Style

Darker cinematic dusk

Use navy-to-midnight gradients with lilac reflections and gold shimmer to restore emotional depth from the original mood board.

Buttons

Gold as intentional signal

Keep gold for booking, confirmation, and premium actions. The dark theme makes gold feel more luxurious, so use it sparingly.

Cards

Light body, dark premium moments

Standard cards stay airy, while select editorial or concierge cards can switch to navy backgrounds for hierarchy.

Identity

The logo belongs in both worlds

Use the full logo on clean surfaces and the monogram in dark hero, favicon, social, and compact application spaces.

Experience

Recommended brand behavior

Website

Luxury first, clean second

Landing pages should begin with atmospheric darkness, then open into cleaner sections for browsing and decision-making.

App / Dashboard

Pastel utility with confident depth

Keep the main UI bright, but allow dark premium widgets for villa status, member privileges, and concierge callouts.

Brand Assets

Luxury collateral with stronger contrast

Tote bags, packaging, signage, and room materials can use navy and midnight backgrounds to feel more upscale and collectible.
